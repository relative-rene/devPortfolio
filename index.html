When do you become a Full-Stack Developer. I suppose I dabbled with role when I was able to design a web application on Wireframes and implement it with JavaScript on a noSQL MongoDB database. I must have basked in my success for all of a minute, before I was in my terminal hitting 'rails new' and began my journey with SQL databases. Several projects later, I question, when do you truly know the full-stack. After 1000+ hours of writing,reviewing and refactoring code, I have come to appreciate Software Engineering as an open source endeavor by necessity. With so much color and variation we need specialist that push the limits of what we know to be possible. A smarter man once said, “The more I learn, the more I realize how much I don't know.” I feel a Full-Stack developer is a person that can contribute in many facets, but is also equiped with the knowledge base to truly appreciate anothers Dev's work. For the unfamiliar, Bob Ross was an artist that hosted a morning show, where the audience got witness him drawing these magnificent landscapes. Regardless, of hobbies, culture or language. Anyone could appreciate a man in front of a canvas, drawing. I never became a great artist, but I am passionate about programming because it has given me a median to create like Bob Ross.
